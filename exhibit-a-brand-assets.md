# OCD Brand Foundation Agreement

**Document ID:** OCD-AGR-001  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Draft — Attorney Review Required  

This OCD Brand Foundation Agreement is entered into by and among:

- **Duy Thai**, individually, solely for the Founder obligations expressly stated herein;
- **Obsessive Cannabis Disorder LLC**, an Oklahoma limited liability company, as Brand Owner;
- **[AUTHORIZED OPERATOR LEGAL NAME]**, an Oklahoma limited liability company, as Authorized Operator; and
- solely where expressly stated, **Flora Summit LLC**, an Oklahoma limited liability company.

## 1. Purpose

The Parties desire to document the OCD brand's ownership, transfer, authorized commercial use, operator relationship, continuity, and supporting records in a clear and commercially reasonable manner.

## 2. Brand Ownership and Assignment

Subject to completion of the attached schedules, Duy Thai assigns to Brand Owner all of his right, title, and interest in the identified OCD Brand Assets, together with the associated goodwill, excluding any rights expressly identified as retained or owned by a third party.

Brand Owner accepts the assignment and confirms that all later licenses or commercial permissions involving the OCD Brand Assets must originate from Brand Owner or its duly authorized representative.

## 3. Authorized Operator License

Brand Owner grants Authorized Operator a limited, non-transferable except as expressly permitted, quality-controlled license to use the OCD Brand Assets solely in connection with lawful activities identified in this Agreement and its exhibits.

Authorized Operator acknowledges that:

- it does not acquire ownership of the OCD Brand Assets merely through use;
- goodwill arising from authorized use benefits Brand Owner;
- a change in cannabis license, processor, distributor, manufacturer, or operating entity does not automatically transfer brand ownership; and
- any sublicense, transfer, or expanded use requires Brand Owner's written authorization.

## 4. Founder and Control Disclosures

Duy Thai and Authorized Operator shall accurately disclose their ownership and control relationship in Exhibit D and promptly update material changes.

Duy Thai personally covenants that he will not knowingly direct, authorize, or participate in a transaction through an entity he controls when the principal purpose or material effect is to defeat the OCD-specific rights expressly granted to Flora Summit LLC under a signed agreement.

This covenant is limited to the OCD brand, its goodwill, and a continuation or successor of the OCD brand. It does not apply merely because Duy Thai later owns or operates a genuinely independent brand.

## 5. Brand Continuity

For contractual purposes, continuity may be established by the totality of circumstances, including:

- migration of OCD goodwill or customers;
- substantially similar branding or commercial presentation;
- public representation that a new mark is a continuation, replacement, or evolution of OCD;
- transfer of the same principal brand assets;
- coordinated discontinuation of OCD followed by substantially similar commercial use; or
- a transaction principally designed to avoid an existing OCD-specific obligation.

Minor changes in spelling, logo, packaging, ownership structure, processor, distributor, or operating entity do not alone terminate existing OCD-specific contractual rights.

## 6. Transfers and Successors

Brand Owner shall not knowingly transfer a material ownership interest in the OCD Brand Assets unless:

1. the transferee receives written notice of then-existing material licenses and exclusive appointments;
2. the transferee executes any assumption required by the applicable agreement; and
3. the transfer documents do not falsely represent that the assets are free of disclosed contractual rights.

A transfer made in breach of this Section constitutes a contractual breach by the transferring Party. Nothing herein purports to bind an unrelated purchaser beyond what applicable law and the purchaser's valid written assumption permit.

## 7. Records and Further Assurances

Each Party shall execute commercially reasonable confirmations, assignments, consents, joinders, assumptions, and recordation documents needed to evidence the intended chain of title and authorized use.

## 8. Representations

Each signing Party represents that:

- it has authority to sign;
- its factual disclosures are materially accurate;
- signing does not knowingly violate another binding agreement; and
- it will not knowingly conceal a material prior ownership claim or transfer restriction.

## 9. Regulatory Separation

This Agreement does not sell, transfer, pledge, or confer ownership of any medical-marijuana license or regulated cannabis business interest except through a separately lawful and properly approved transaction.

## 10. General Terms

The final execution version must include approved provisions addressing governing law, venue, notices, amendments, counterparts, electronic signatures, severability, waiver, integration, and remedies.
