# OCD Brand Foundation Agreement

**Document ID:** OCD-OWN-002 / OCD-LIC-001  
**Version:** 1.0.0  
**Status:** Draft — Attorney Review Required

This Agreement is entered into as of **[EFFECTIVE DATE]** among:

- **Duy Thai**, individually, solely for obligations expressly imposed on the Founder;
- **Obsessive Cannabis Disorder LLC**, an Oklahoma limited liability company (“Brand Owner”); and
- **[AUTHORIZED OPERATOR LEGAL NAME]**, an Oklahoma limited liability company (“Authorized Operator”).

## 1. Commercial Purpose

The Parties wish to establish a clear chain of title for the OCD brand, place identified Brand Assets into Brand Owner, authorize appropriate use by Authorized Operator, preserve quality and goodwill, and prevent confusion between brand ownership and regulated cannabis-business licensing.

## 2. Defined Terms

**Brand Assets** means the OCD and Obsessive Cannabis Disorder names, logos, icons, packaging identifiers, trade dress, slogans, source artwork, domains, social identifiers, marketing materials, and associated goodwill specifically listed in the exhibits.

**Controlled Entity** means an entity directly or indirectly controlled by a Party. Control means the power to direct material management or policies through ownership, agreement, or comparable authority.

**OCD Brand** means the Brand Assets and associated goodwill, including a continuation or successor determined under Section 9.

## 3. Founder Assignment

Subject to disclosed exceptions, Duy Thai hereby assigns to Brand Owner all of his right, title, and interest in the Brand Assets, together with the goodwill associated with the business symbolized by those assets.

The assignment does not include:

- an OMMA license;
- ownership of a licensed cannabis business;
- regulated inventory;
- licensed premises;
- third-party property disclosed in the exhibits; or
- rights Duy Thai does not own and has accurately disclosed.

## 4. Brand Owner Acceptance

Brand Owner accepts the assignment and confirms that future brand licenses, permissions, and transfers must originate from Brand Owner or a duly authorized representative.

Brand Owner shall maintain records reasonably sufficient to show its chain of title.

## 5. Authorized Operator License

Brand Owner grants Authorized Operator a limited, revocable only as provided herein, non-exclusive, non-transferable except as expressly approved, quality-controlled license to use the OCD Brand for lawful production, processing, packaging, distribution, marketing, and related activities within the scope of Authorized Operator's valid licenses and approvals.

Authorized Operator acknowledges:

1. it does not obtain ownership of the OCD Brand merely through use;
2. goodwill generated by authorized use benefits Brand Owner;
3. it may not assign or sublicense brand rights without written authorization;
4. it must follow quality-control and brand-use requirements;
5. expiration, suspension, sale, or transfer of its cannabis license does not automatically transfer the OCD Brand; and
6. the brand license does not authorize any unlawful cannabis activity.

## 6. Quality Control

Brand Owner may establish reasonable quality and brand-use standards. Authorized Operator shall cooperate with commercially reasonable review of packaging, labeling, consumer-facing use, and brand consistency.

## 7. Founder and Control Disclosures

Duy Thai and Authorized Operator shall disclose their ownership and control relationship and promptly update material changes.

Duy Thai personally represents that the disclosure is materially accurate as of execution.

## 8. Limited Founder Covenant

Duy Thai personally covenants that he will not knowingly direct, fund, authorize, or participate in using a Controlled Entity, nominee, intermediary, undisclosed side arrangement, or related operator for the principal purpose or material effect of defeating OCD-specific contractual rights validly granted by Brand Owner.

This covenant:

- is limited to the OCD Brand and its goodwill;
- does not create personal liability for ordinary company debts;
- does not prohibit Duy Thai from owning or operating genuinely independent brands; and
- does not prevent lawful transactions that honor disclosed OCD obligations.

## 9. Brand Continuity

A brand may be treated contractually as a continuation, successor, replacement, or material evolution of OCD based on the totality of circumstances, including:

- migration of OCD goodwill, customers, accounts, or campaigns;
- public statements that the new brand continues or replaces OCD;
- transfer of principal OCD artwork, packaging, slogans, or identifiers;
- coordinated discontinuation and relaunch;
- substantially similar commercial identity combined with transferred goodwill; or
- a restructuring principally intended to avoid an existing OCD obligation.

A shared owner, processor, distributor, product type, or market alone is insufficient.

## 10. Transfers

Brand Owner shall not knowingly transfer material Brand Assets without:

1. disclosing then-existing material licenses and exclusive appointments;
2. accurately scheduling those obligations in the transaction;
3. obtaining an assumption where required by the applicable agreement and legally available; and
4. refraining from representing that the assets are free of disclosed rights.

Nothing here claims to bind an unrelated purchaser beyond applicable law or a valid assumption. A noncompliant transfer remains a breach by the transferring Party.

## 11. Regulatory Separation

Nothing in this Agreement transfers an OMMA license, licensed business ownership, regulated inventory, or another regulated interest. A regulated change must be separately reviewed and completed under applicable law and OMMA procedures.

## 12. Representations

Each Party represents that:

- it has authority to sign;
- its material disclosures are accurate;
- it has not knowingly concealed a conflicting agreement;
- execution does not knowingly violate a binding obligation; and
- it will provide commercially reasonable further assurances.

## 13. Indemnity

The final execution version should allocate responsibility for undisclosed ownership claims, unauthorized use, regulatory violations, and breaches in a manner approved by counsel.

## 14. Term and Termination

The brand assignment is permanent subject to applicable law and any valid rescission rights.

The Authorized Operator license continues until terminated under the final counsel-approved termination provisions, which should address material breach, loss of required license, illegality, insolvency, uncured quality failures, and mutual written agreement.

Termination of the operator license does not reverse the Founder assignment.

## 15. General Terms

Final counsel-approved terms must address notices, amendments, assignment, waiver, integration, severability, counterparts, electronic signatures, governing law, venue, dispute resolution, remedies, and attorneys' fees.
